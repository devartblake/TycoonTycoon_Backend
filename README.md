# Synaptix

Synaptix is a cognitive arcade and competition experience built around fast trivia, skill growth, missions, rewards, and social play.

This public README is intentionally product-facing. Internal architecture, operations, security, deployment, and administration details are kept in private staff documentation.

---

## What Players Do

Synaptix lets players:

- Answer timed questions across multiple categories.
- Compete in live and asynchronous challenges.
- Build streaks, earn XP, and climb seasonal ranks.
- Unlock missions, rewards, cosmetics, and progression bonuses.
- Study and improve through learning-focused experiences.
- Play with friends, follow leaderboards, and participate in events.

The experience is designed to feel quick, competitive, rewarding, and skill-driven.

---

## Core Gameplay

### Trivia And Challenges

Players answer questions, build score, and improve their mastery across topics. Competitive gameplay is built around fair, validated outcomes for scoring, rewards, and ranks.

### Missions

Missions guide players toward daily, weekly, seasonal, and event-based goals. Examples include streak goals, category goals, comeback goals, and mastery goals.

### Rewards

Players can earn XP, coins, diamonds, cosmetics, rank progress, and event rewards. Reward experiences are designed to be exciting without becoming real-money gambling mechanics.

### Progression

Progression is built around long-term player growth:

- XP and level growth
- Rank and tier advancement
- Seasonal goals
- Skill-tree style bonuses
- Collection and cosmetic unlocks

---

## Learning And Improvement

Synaptix separates competitive play from learning-focused practice.

Learning experiences can include:

- Study sessions
- Practice questions
- Favorites and review flows
- Topic improvement loops
- Coach-style guidance

Competitive gameplay does not expose answer keys during live play. Learning modes may show explanations when the goal is education rather than competition.

---

## Personalization

Synaptix adapts parts of the experience to help players stay engaged and improve. Personalization is used for things like mission suggestions, learning recommendations, and safer player guidance.

Personalization is not intended to create unfair competitive advantages. Ranked and competitive flows remain fairness-first.

---

## Store And Cosmetics

The store supports player-facing customization and optional progression items. Cosmetics, avatars, bundles, and event items can be presented through rotating catalog experiences.

Purchases and rewards are checked before they affect a player account.

---

## Events And Seasons

Synaptix supports seasonal play, limited-time events, and leaderboard-driven goals. Events can introduce special missions, rewards, themes, and competition windows.

---

## Safety And Fair Play

Synaptix is built around fair competition and protected player progress.

The platform includes safeguards for:

- Account ownership
- Reward validation
- Duplicate-action protection
- Moderation workflows
- Anti-cheat review
- Secure handling of sensitive actions

Detailed security implementation information is not published in this README.
